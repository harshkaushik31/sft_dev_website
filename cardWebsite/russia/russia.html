About Russia

Russia, the largest country in the world by land area, spans Eastern Europe and northern Asia, covering over 17 million square kilometers and 11 time zones. Known for its diverse landscapes, Russia is home to vast forests, tundras, mountain ranges, and over 100,000 rivers. The country’s capital, Moscow, is a cultural and political center, while St. Petersburg, known for its historic architecture and cultural landmarks, is another major city.

With a rich history and deep cultural heritage, Russia has contributed significantly to global literature, music, arts, and sciences. The Russian language, one of the most widely spoken Slavic languages, is integral to its national identity. Russia’s historical roots stretch back to medieval Kievan Rus and the Tsardom of Russia, later becoming a major world power as part of the Soviet Union in the 20th century. Today, Russia is known for its energy exports, technological advancements in space exploration, and strong influence in international politics. The country’s unique mix of European and Asian cultural influences makes it a fascinating place for travelers and scholars alike.
