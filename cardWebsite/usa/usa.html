About USA

The United States of America (USA), commonly known as the United States (U.S. or US) or America, is a country primarily located in North America. It consists of 50 states, a federal district, five major unincorporated territories, 326 Indian reservations, and some minor possessions. The capital city is Washington, D.C., and the most populous city is New York City.

The United States is known for its diverse geography, climate, and wildlife. It is a melting pot of cultures, with a rich history and a significant influence on global politics, economy, and culture. The country is renowned for its technological advancements, educational institutions, and cultural landmarks.

1776

Declaration of Independence

The United States declared independence from Great Britain on July 4, 1776. This historic event marked the birth of a new nation, founded on principles of liberty, equality, and democracy. The Declaration of Independence, primarily authored by Thomas Jefferson, is a seminal document in American history.

1865

End of Civil War

The American Civil War ended in 1865, leading to the abolition of slavery. This conflict, fought between the Northern states (Union) and the Southern states (Confederacy), was one of the most significant events in American history, reshaping the nation's social and political landscape.

1969

Moon Landing

NASA's Apollo 11 mission successfully landed the first humans on the Moon in 1969. Astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin made history with their lunar walk, symbolizing human achievement and the spirit of exploration. This event remains a milestone in space exploration.

2001

9/11 Attacks

The September 11 attacks in 2001 had a profound impact on the United States and the world. Coordinated terrorist attacks by the extremist group al-Qaeda targeted the World Trade Center and the Pentagon, leading to significant loss of life and changes in global security policies.
